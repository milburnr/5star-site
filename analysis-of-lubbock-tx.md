## **1\) Hero & Above-the-Fold (ATF)**

**What it is now**

* The headline “Lubbock’s Premier Roofing Contractor” reads like a brochure.  
* Call button is present, but buried among text visually.  
* No immediate reinforcement of local **pain**, **primary benefit**, or **simple proposition** before the scroll.

**What high-conversion landing pages do**

* A single powerful, *benefit-driven* headline (e.g., “Free Same-Day Lubbock Roof Inspections After Severe Hail”) that matches intent.  
* Subheadline clarifies *what happens next*.  
* A **prominent phone CTA \+ simple form** above the fold. ([GetResponse](https://www.getresponse.com/blog/roofing-landing-page?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

**Why this matters**  
Visitors decide in \~5 seconds whether they’ll engage or bounce. If they don’t *feel understood* immediately, they leave — regardless of great content below.

## **2\) Primary Call to Action (CTA)**

**Current state**

* CTA exists, but it’s visually the same weight as navigation links and text content.  
* The CTAs are all over the page with no design hierarchy.

**Best practice principle**

* Primary CTA above the fold should be the *only* big button (phone \+ form). Secondary CTAs (learn more) come later after trust is built. ([roofing10x.com](https://roofing10x.com/7-landing-page-design-tips-to-maximize-roofing-ppc-conversions/?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

**Tradeoff**

* Right now it feels educational. That’s good for SEO, bad for conversion. You need a *conversion-first version* of this page with the content *after* the CTA, not before it.

**Concrete fix example**

* Above fold:  
  **Headline:** “Free Lubbock Roof Inspection – We Handle Hail Damage & Insurance Claims.”  
  **CTA row:** Big call button \+ 2-field form (Name, Phone).  
  **Subtext:** “No Sales Pressure. Local Storm Experts.”

## **7\) Structural Recommendation (Concrete)**

You can keep the existing content — but it should be *second-tier* to a **conversion-optimized frame**.

**Priority Structure**

1. Hero with urgency \+ CTA  
2. Proof elements (reviews, local awards, years)  
3. Pain points (hail, leaks) *briefly*  
4. Lead form \+ phone  
5. Services with short blurbs \+ secondary CTAs  
6. Detailed long content (climate, neighborhoods, insurance)

Right now, sections 3 and 6 dominate the first screen → opportunity cost in lost leads.
